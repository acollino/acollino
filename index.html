About

Throughout high school and college, I was interested in both biology and computer science, but ultimately ended up pursuing a biology major. I graduated from Brandeis University in 2015 with the goal of attending medical school, and spent several years working as an anesthesia technician to get more experience in the hospital environment. I was eventually accepted to the Geisel School of Medicine at Dartmouth College in 2017, and I believed I was set to achieve my dreams and become a doctor. However, that proved not to be the case. I struggled to be the version of myself I felt that I needed to be - when I made mistakes, I should have studied harder. If I was enjoying myself, I shouldn't let myself get distracted. I found I was cutting ties with friends old and new, dropping hobbies I used to love, and despite it all - I still wasn't good enough. This was supposed to be what I wanted to be doing with my life, and I was miserable. So I requested a leave of absence from Geisel, moved to be with my partner in LA, and I'm now exploring what my future could look like if I shifted my focus towards software development. I've been progressing through The Odin Project's Foundations and Javascript paths to solidify the basics of HTML, CSS, and Javascript, and I'm currently enrolled in Springboard's Software Engineering course. I've also resumed some of my hobbies; feel free to ask me about baking bread or nerd out with me over D&D, MtG, or DotA!
